Maize - The Healthy Staple

Maize is a nutritious, versatile crop packed with health benefits. It plays a vital role in British and Ghanaian cuisine.

Health Benefits

Maize is rich in essential nutrients, including fibre, antioxidants, and vitamins. It supports heart health, helps regulate blood sugar, and provides a great source of energy for the body.

Types of Maize in Ghana

In Ghana, maize is grown in various forms, each suitable for different culinary purposes. The major types of maize include:

White Maize

White maize is the most commonly used variety in Ghana. It is typically ground into flour to make dishes such as Banku and Kenkey. It is also used for porridge and as a side dish to accompany meats and stews. White maize is known for its smooth texture and slightly sweet flavour.

Yellow Maize

Yellow maize is often used in Ghana for making cornmeal and in traditional dishes like "koko" (fermented maize porridge). The colour comes from its higher beta-carotene content, which provides a rich source of Vitamin A. Yellow maize is also used in the production of maize flour, cornflakes, and animal feed.

Broken Maize

Broken maize refers to maize kernels that have been broken into smaller pieces. It is commonly used to make maize flour for dishes such as Banku and Kenkey. Broken maize is also a popular ingredient in the preparation of "koko" (fermented porridge), a traditional breakfast dish in Ghana.

Ghanaian Dishes from Maize

Porridge (Koko)

Maize is used in Ghana to prepare a popular breakfast dish called "porridge" or "koko". This porridge is made from ground maize flour and is typically served with milk, sugar, or honey. It is a warm, filling dish that provides a great start to the day.

Banku

Banku is a staple Ghanaian dish made from white maize and fermented cassava dough. The mixture is stirred to create a smooth, stretchy dough-like consistency. It is often paired with fish, soup, or stew, making it a nutritious and hearty meal.

Kenkey

Kenkey is a traditional Ghanaian dish made from fermented maize dough. It is wrapped in corn husks and boiled until fully cooked. Kenkey is commonly eaten with soup, stew, or fried fish, and is a favourite dish in coastal Ghana.
